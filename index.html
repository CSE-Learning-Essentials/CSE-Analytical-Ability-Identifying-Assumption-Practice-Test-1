Created by: CSE Learning Essentials

Section 2: Analytical Ability - Part 4: Identifying Assumptions

Practice Test 1

Passing Score: 4

Directions: Each item in this section consists of a statement or a passage followed by several assumptions. Determine the best assumption that can be logically made from the given statement or passage.

1) A woman enters a room and sees a man sitting on the floor, shaking and looking terrified. The room is dark, and the only source of light is a dim, flickering candle. The woman quickly turns on the light, and the man immediately calms down. What is the correct assumption?

  • A) The man was afraid of the dark.
  • B) The man was afraid of the light.
  • C) The man was waiting for the woman to turn on the light.
  • D) The man was just pretending to be scared.
  • E) The man was afraid of the woman.

2) Statement: Regular exercise is important for maintaining a healthy lifestyle.

Assumption I: People who exercise regularly are less likely to develop health issues.
Assumption II: People who do not exercise at all are unhealthy.
Assumption III: Exercise has physical and mental health benefits.

  • A) Only Assumption I follows
  • B) Both Assumptions I & III follow
  • C) Assumptions I, II & III follow
  • D) Only Assumption III follows
  • E) None of the Above

3) Statement: Rizal is a patriotic and intelligent Filipino, so he should be a Philippine national hero.

Assumption I: A Philippine national hero died fighting for the country.
Assumption II: Patriotism and intelligence are essential attributes of being a national hero.
Assumption III: When it comes to weapons and firearms, the Philippines is worth dying for.

  • A) Only Assumption I follows
  • B) Only Assumptions II follows
  • C) Only Assumption III follows
  • D) Assumption I and III follow
  • E) None of the Above

4) Statement: Technology plays a significant role in education today.

Assumption I: Teachers can teach more effectively with the help of technology.
Assumption II: Students who use technology in education will always perform better academically.
Assumption III: Technology in education can make learning more interactive and engaging.

  • A) Only Assumption I follows
  • B) Assumptions I & III follow
  • C) Assumptions I, II & III follow
  • D) Only Assumption II follows
  • E) None of the Above

5) The Philippine National Police has decided to cancel the summer camp training this year with a required number of expected participants.

  • A) It is being cancelled because the weather is too hot
  • B) The cancellation is due to a shortage of funds for the summer camp training.
  • C) The PNP has decided to postpone the summer camp training until next year.
  • D) No entries have been received by the PNP for officers willing to join the summer camp training.
  • E) The PNP has cancelled the summer camp because they want to organize a different type of training instead.
